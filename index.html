Design Research · DMD 3720

The Designer of Glossier

What interested me most about Glossier was not the pink or the minimalism but the philosophy underneath it. Adriana Deleo described their approach as a balance between the aspirational and the practical, designing for the medicine cabinet rather than the store shelf.

This project was an attempt to understand what that actually means when translated into a font choice, a packaging decision, or a product name. The presentation was built entirely inside Glossier's own visual language as a way of testing that understanding.

Digital Design Lab · Poster Design

Digi-Phernia
Exhibition Posters

Three posters designed for the 2024 UConn Digital Media and Design MFA Thesis Exhibition at the William Benton Museum of Art. Each poster interprets a different corner of digital space: a computer window, a digital paint canvas, and a video player, using pixelated type and primary colors to evoke a retro digital aesthetic.

Inspired by Douglas Rushkoff's concept of "digi-phernia," the phenomenon of navigating multiple digital realms simultaneously, the posters connect nostalgia for early digital culture with the present creative freedom of shared digital spaces.

Interactive — p5.js

Digital Nostalgia
An Interactive Memory

Built for an intro to programming course using JavaScript in p5.js, this piece is a personal meditation on digital nostalgia. Clicking anywhere on the canvas cycles through 50 personal photos and childhood screenshots, faces blurred in Photoshop for that hazy, half-remembered quality. A starfield follows the cursor. The whole thing feels like playing a computer game in the early 2000s.

Inspired by net art references including Chia Amisola's "We are only moving towards each other" and Sarah Rothberg's "MEMORY/PLACE: MY HOUSE."
